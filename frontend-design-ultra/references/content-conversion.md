# Content And Conversion

Design quality collapses when copy is vague. Treat copy as UI.

## Page Job

Every page needs:

- one audience
- one promise
- one proof path
- one primary action
- one reason to trust

If these are unclear, fix them before styling.

## Hero Contract

- H1: position or literal offer.
- Subline: concrete result, not abstract adjectives.
- CTA: says what happens next.
- Secondary action: only if it has different intent.
- Proof: visible within first scroll, not hidden in footer.

Hero constraints:

- headline usually 2 lines max on desktop
- subline around 20 words unless the layout explicitly supports more
- one primary CTA plus at most one secondary CTA
- no logo wall, pricing teaser, feature bullet list, or trust micro-strip inside the hero unless it is the central concept

## CTA Discipline

- One label per intent across nav, hero, body, footer.
- No duplicate contact/signup/view-work labels with different wording.
- CTA text should fit on one line on desktop.
- Ghost buttons over images need scrim, stroke, or solid contrast.

## Copy Self-Audit

Before shipping, reread every visible string:

- remove filler verbs: elevate, unleash, seamless, next-gen, revolutionary
- remove cute-but-wrong metaphors
- remove fake humility or poetic micro-copy that does not explain anything
- replace fake-precise numbers unless sourced or clearly marked sample
- keep one copy register per page

## Testimonials And Trust

- Quote body should fit at a glance.
- Attribution needs name, role, and company when possible.
- Trust blocks go next to friction: CTA, pricing, checkout, forms, enterprise proof.
- Do not invent awards, customer numbers, or brand logos as real proof.

## E-Commerce

Product detail first scan band:

- product media
- product name
- price
- variant choice
- shipping/returns confidence
- primary CTA

Move long descriptions and specs into accordions, grouped chunks, or visual tiles. Do not ship long spec tables as default.
